Ready-to-use Fourier domain templates for compact binaries inspiraling alongmoderately eccentric orbits
Paper published: 2019

We derive analytic expressions that provide Fourier domain gravitational wave (GW) response function for compact binaries inspiraling along moderately eccentric orbits. These expressions include amplitude corrections to the two GW polarization states that are accurate to the first post- Newtonian (PN) order. Additionally, our fully 3PN accurate GW phase evolution incorporates eccentricity effects upto sixth order at each PN order. Further, we develop a prescription to incorporate analytically the effects of 3PN accurate periastron advance in the GW phase evolution. This is how we provide a ready-to-use and effcient inspiral template family for compact binaries in moderately eccentric orbits.


Geometrical source localization of Gravitational waves.
M.Sc. project write-up: 2018

A simple geometrical technique can be used for Gravitational wave (GW) source localization using the time delay information between the detectors. Associated uncertainty can be derived, and within its bound it can be shown that the result is consistent with the LIGO information. Further study of the uncertainty and resolution has shown the necessity of multiple detectors. A proper statistical method is also presented for location determination and its uncertainty.


Measurement of Time Period of a simple pendulum using an electronic circuit
Paper published: 2015

This project was taken up in the hope of building an electronic circuit which enables us to measure the time period of a simple pendulum accurately, taking into account the parallax, human reflex and random errors. In this project an attempt is made to detect the pendulum using a laser detector with which a circuit consisting of timers and counters is employed to measure the number of oscillations and time of the journey simultaneously. The required result is allowed to display using 7447 IC and SSD (seven segment display). Thus, a cost effective and highly accurate design is obtained using concepts which are well familiar with and read by any college student pursuing Bsc. Physics Hons.


In The Realm of Bose (The diary of a teenagers brief sojourn at Bose Institute)
Book published: 2012

This book is about my experience relating to The Winter Science Training Programme at Bose Institute, Kolkata, India (Dec2008). The program is mainly initiated to create scientific temperament to young school going students of India. Various lectures in the field of physics, chemistry and biology were given. Also the participants were introduced to various scientific hands on activities and experiments. During that time I kept a diary with myself to record the activities of the program which lasted 10 days. I later wrote it out as a book and presented in Bose Institute (Kolkata) with the help of the prof. Gautam Basu. The book is not just a straight forward conveyance of the activities, but also a philosophical portrayal of keen insight of things surrounding a curious young mind.
